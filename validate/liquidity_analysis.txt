                              ASSESSMENT                            
====================================================================

  The two completed analogues bracket a 3–6 year adoption window:

  Factors favouring the faster (variance swap) scenario:
    • Digital regime classification is binary — no continuous valuation
      curve to bootstrap, unlike inflation or credit curves.
    • The GMRI classification algorithm is fully transparent and
      reproducible from public FRED data, reducing barrier to price-
      checking and dealer adoption.
    • Institutional demand for macro regime hedges (risk parity,
      CTA, multi-asset) is well-established.

  Factors favouring the slower (inflation swap) scenario:
    • No existing exchange-listed product to anchor daily settlement.
    • Requires standardized ISDA confirmation language for the GMRI
      index definition, growth signal, and regime classification rules.
    • Limited natural two-way flow until both long-inflation-regime
      and short-inflation-regime constituencies are identified.

  GDP-warrant tail risk:
    • If the instrument attracts no natural hedging constituency
      (i.e., corporates/sovereigns with P&L materially linked to
      regime duration), liquidity may never consolidate, as with
      the Greece GDP warrants that remain orphaned 14+ years after
      issuance.

  Conclusion:
    Base estimate: widespread quoting achievable by ~2030
    Confidence interval: 2029–2032
    Preconditions: ISDA template, at least 2 dealer market-makers,
    and index governance body publishing monthly regime determinations.
