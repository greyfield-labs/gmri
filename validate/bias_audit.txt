  BIAS 1 OF 5 — LOOK-AHEAD BIAS
========================================================================

  Question: Does each month's regime classification use only data
  available as of that month's publication date?

  Publication lag requirements tested:
    CPI (CPIAUCSL): must have ≥14-day lag
    GDP (GDPC1):    must have ≥30-day lag

  FRED series publication schedule:
  Series      Agency  Lag           Note
  ----------------------------------------------------------------------
  CPIAUCSL    BLS     14 days      Released ~12–15 business days after reference month end
  UNRATE      BLS      5 days      Released first Friday after reference month end (~5 days)
  GDPC1       BEA     30 days      Advance estimate released ~30 days after quarter end
  NROU        CBO     90 days      CBO publishes annual/semi-annual updates; ~60–90 day lag
  GDPPOT      CBO     90 days      CBO publishes annual/semi-annual updates; ~60–90 day lag

  Findings:

  (a) CPI look-ahead:
      GMRI uses same-month CPIAUCSL for CPI YoY — zero real-time lag.
      At month-end M, CPI for month M is not published for ~14 more days.
      Affected months: 657/657 (100%) — ALL months if used in real-time.
      Impact: For historical back-test, no effect (data exists).
      For real-time deployment, each month's signal is delayed ~14 days.

  (b) GDP in-quarter look-ahead:
      GDPC1 is quarterly. For month M in quarter Q, the advance GDP
      estimate is only available ~30 days after Q ends.
      Months using GDP before it is published: 657/657 (100.0%)
      Affected months: Jan, Feb of Q1; Apr, May of Q2; Jul, Aug of Q3;
      Oct, Nov of Q4 (i.e., first 2 months of each quarter).

  (c) Cubic spline look-ahead (structural):
      Interpolating quarterly GDP to monthly with cubic spline uses
      FUTURE quarterly observations to smooth the in-quarter path.
      Affected months: 438/657 (66.7%) — all non-quarter-end months.
      Severity: mild. Spline interpolation of GDP trend changes the
      within-quarter path but rarely changes the sign of the GDP gap.

  VULNERABILITY:  △ MEDIUM

  Evidence: All three look-ahead channels identified and quantified.
  The historical back-test uses realized data throughout — look-ahead
  does not affect the 50-year regime classification itself. It affects
  the REAL-TIME applicability of the regime signal.

  Recommended mitigation:
    1. For real-time use: publish each month's regime determination
       ~15 days after month-end (after CPI release).
    2. For GDP: use prior-quarter GDP only (freeze the interpolation
       at the last confirmed quarterly observation).
    3. Disclose in white paper that real-time regime lag = 14–30 days.
